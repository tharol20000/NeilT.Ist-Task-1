Question 3. Intranet and relate it to the internet

An intranet is a private network that is owned by a certain company. The company’s employees have access to the intranet and it allows to them to collaborate, communicate and get informed on matters relating to the company. It can be a website or computers that are connected on the company’s network. Each company's intranet will be different according to their objectives, structure and what they do, but they all have elements in common. These include; a system that holds and manages confidential or necessary information and documents so that the employees can access them; a way for employees to communicate with each other, especially if they are far in physical distance, forums and discussion areas that can enable employees to ask questions to a wide audience, get reliable answers, and give answers of their own to inform others; and specific forms or transactions that can be completed online such as booking holidays. The difference between the internet and the intranet is that the intranet is on a much smaller scale than the internet. The internet can be accessed by anyone whereas the intranet can only be accessible to employees of a business usually through a special login and password. On the internet anyone can request access to any document while the intranet only people with a login can access it. It is much safer than the internet.

Question 4. What is an internet protocol? Compare HTTP, ICP/IP and FTP

Internet Protocol is the procedure in which through data is sent from one computer to another across the internet. Every single computer, phone and device that can connect to the internet has its very own unique 32-digit IP Address that distinguishes it from every other device on the internet and that allows for it to be easily found so that data can be transferred to or from it. HTTP (Hyper Text Transfer Protocol) is a type of Internet Protocol, something on your computer makes a request with a server to which the server acknowledges and responds.. It is optimal for transferring hypertext documents across systems. TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) is a transport protocol which means it dictates the method by which data is received and transferred to places. It sends the data in smaller portions called packets which are reassembled at its destination. Each packet has an IP Address which helps it travel through systems until it reaches its destination. TCP first connects with the destination of the data and then sends all the packets, of which the recipient will acknowledge. If the recipient does not acknowledge a packet, TCP sends it again, and since TCP/IP has to wait for every package to be sent and received, it can take a longer time. TCP is optimal for reliability, but not speed. It is also the most popular protocol. FTP (File Transfer Protocol) allows an user to transfer files from one device to another device. You can download and upload many different types of files, for example, pictures, documents, there are many more. Websites that have applications that you can download typically use this protocol. All these protocols are used for different things; HTTP to send HTML files; TCP/IP to transport the data; and FTP to download and upload data, and they are necessary for certain things when accessing the world wide web through the internet
